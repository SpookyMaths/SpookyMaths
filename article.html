How are transformations of functions used in real life?

There are a whole bunch of ways in which transformations of functions can be used in real life. Here is an example: Let us say that you have a graph representing your companies' profits over time. The current function of this graph is $$f\left(x\right)=\sqrt{x}$$



Now what if you want to see what the companies' profits would look life if they tripled. Simply multiply by 3. Now you have $$f\left(x\right)=3\sqrt{x}$$



and you can now see what the profits would look like tripled on the graph.

Word Problems

Your companies' profits over the past few years can be represented by the function $$f(x)=|x|$$



With the x-axis being years and the y-axis being dollars in millions. Your boss wants you to find out what would happen if the company started off with an extra $3 million. You must find the function and the graph.

Answer To Word Problem

$$f(x)=|x|$$ $$f(x)=|x|+3$$





Word Problem 2

Your friends' bank account over the past few weeks can be represented by the function $$f\left(x\right)=\sqrt{x}$$



With the x-axis being weeks and the y-axis being dollars. Your friend wants you to find out what would happen if the bank account was opened five weeks ago and started with 2 dollars. You must find the function and the graph.

Answer To Word Problem 2

$$f\left(x\right)=\sqrt{x}$$ $$f\left(x\right)=\sqrt{x+5}$$ $$f\left(x\right)=\sqrt{x+5}+2$$
